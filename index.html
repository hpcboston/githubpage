Competiton Overview

HPC Boston is a 2-day competition for undergraduate students. Student teams must design and build a system that can execute typical HPC workloads. Teams are scored primarily on the ability to complete the provided workloads at the best price/performance.

Team Information
  1. Teams must consist of 2-4 currently-enrolled undergraduate students. Each student team may have one faculty or graduate student advisor. Once the competition starts, students may not consult advisors for any technical questions.
  2. All team members must be available to attend from 8am to 8pm on November 9th and 8am to 12pm on November 10th
  3. During the duration of the competition, food will be provided to students. Teams are strongly encouraged to find sponsorship for their hardware and transportation costs.

Hardware Specifications

There is a $5,000 cap on hardware expenses for the team. All relevant hardware (including but not limited to cases and cables) must be under this budget. Hardware must be purchased new and from retailers that are accessible to everyone.

During the application process, teams will have an opportunity to submit a hardware specification. This hardware specification list may change up until two weeks before the start of the competition. At this time, a final specification list must be submitted along with an accurate budget. ALL hardware purchased for the final system (including but not limited to cases and cables) must be included in this budget.

This budget must include the following information for all components used in the final competition system:

  • Location purchased
  • Price of equipment
  • Quantity
Once the competition begins, the specifications of a system cannot be changed. In the event that a component fails during the competition, hardware may be replaced for the exact same hardware.


Judging and Prizes

Team will be mainly scored on completion of workloads as well as HPCG scores. A prize for the best team will be awarded.

Workloads

During the competition, students will be running a diverse set of workloads.

Preliminary workloads

Teams will be required to run HPCG Benchmark, an industry standard benchmark designed to profile paralle systems.

In addition, teams will be required to develop the below programs:

  • A graph-based search application
  • A document string-search application
  • A clustering-based application

Finally, a mystery application will be announed at the day of the competition.

A detailed description of each workload will be posted by October 5th

Applications and Logistics

The priority deadline for applications is October 5th, 2019.
Applications will be accepted on a rolling basis after and will close when there is no more space.

Applications are submitted through Typeform, a 3rd party service. Along with basic biographic information, a 1 to 2 page proposal must be attached.

Each proposal must include the following information:

  1. Preliminary hardware information
    • Basic hardware specifications, such as processor types, RAM, storage, number of nodes/system, etc.
    • How will your team optimize your system for the best price to performance ratio?
  2. Team member background information
    • Past experience of team members (including internships, co-ops, and researach experience)
    • How will you use your past experience to succeed in this competition?
  3. Vendor/institution support
  4. Plan for workload implementions
